Systematic review on the
risks of diabetes complications

Research Systematic review Epidemiology

Patients living with diabetes are at greater risk of developing complications, Therefore, several teams of researchers have tried to create statistical tools to estimate the risk of complications based on several variables, such as age, gender, type of diabetes, etc. However, these tools are often limited, for example they are sometimes made with cohorts representing only the local population, or they only evalutate the risks of developing a specific complication.

So a team, of which I was a member, has reviewed the scientific literature on this subject in order to synthesize the knowledge that the scientific community possesses and then make conclusions about it. In this project, I sorted the articles from the scientific databases and I extracted data from their statistical risk estimation models for further analysis. The project was presented at the SMDM congress and a paper was submitted afterwards.
